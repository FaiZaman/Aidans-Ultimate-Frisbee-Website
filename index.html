A brief history and summary of Aidan's Ultimate Frisbee by the A-team captain


Ultimate Frisbee is one of the fastest growing sports in the country and the Aidan's college team [SACUF] has an exciting and successful history within this trend.

The vast majority of people involved in Ultimate first get involved at University, so don't worry if you have never heard of it before. Ultimate is a 7vs7 mixed-sex sport that combines agility, athleticism, grace, and ridiculous amounts of fun. For the more official rules, look here, and for some hardcore footage, look here.

Ultimate is wonderfully unique in that it actively encourages sportmanship, in what is called 'spirit of the game', or Spirit for short. At the end of games teams will score each other on things like sportmanship, restraint of physical contact, knowledge of rules and mutual respect. These scores are taken to be of equal importance to the points scored within the game. This, combined with the self-refereed nature of the sport, fosters a special sporting atmosphere that is often clearly amiss in other modern games but helps to shape ultimate as not only a sport but as a national and even international community.

Traditionally the chilled out participant of the college leagues even by ultimate frisbee's standards, the Aidan's team nonetheless consistently performs well, especially in the annual College Cup which we had won for two years running till the heathen Grey college managed to beat us in the final last year. I'm sure that this year will be different with your new invovlement.

As well as the sporting side of the club, SACUF is also a close-knit team and the social aspect of the club is naturally very important. Our social secretaries have an outstanding tradition of great socials that cater to all tastes, be they in the most chillaxed barbeques by the riverside in the luscious Durham summer evenings, or in consistently very tasteful and definitely law-abidingly outrageous bar crawls. Some of my best moments in Durham have been had with the college ultimate team, on and off the pitch, and I'm sure some of yours will be too.

The college team also has great ties with the university team [DUF] and Aidan's players have taken various roles in DUF's executive committee, including last year's captain and presidents of DUF. Some of the most able players have played at international levels for GB. Who knows, if you wanted, you too could reach these standards some day - the only way to find out is to join us and see!
